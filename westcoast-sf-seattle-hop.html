Stay in Hayes Valley: quiet, walkable, boutiques and bars, near the Muni metro.

A bay ferry to cathedral redwoods, then a harbor lunch facing the skyline.

Golden Gate Ferry from the Ferry Building to Sausalito (~30 min) — the ride itself is half the day, skyline and bridge views.
Muir Woods: take the Muir Woods Shuttle (Route 66) from Sausalito — a parking reservation at gomuirwoods.com is REQUIRED; book ahead.
The redwoods are an easy, flat boardwalk loop through Cathedral and Bohemian Groves — cool, hushed, ancient. No real climbing.
Sausalito waterfront: lunch on the harbor, gelato, galleries, the houseboats — relaxed downtime with SF across the water.
Car-free verdict: very doable. Lazier option — skip Muir and just ferry to Sausalito for a long lunch.

Wine Country Sonoma · Napa

Sun-warmed vines, slow pours, harvest in the air — the trip's pure downtime day.

Best car-free option: a small-group guided tour with SF hotel pickup (~$139pp, 3 backroad wineries + picnic lunch). A designated driver means everyone tastes freely.
For this crew, pick Sonoma over Napa — laid-back, less crowded, family-owned, walk-ins welcome, 30+ walkable tasting rooms on the historic Plaza. Napa is more polished but pricier and busier.
A relaxed anchor: Gundlach Bundschu, California's oldest family winery — a lakeside patio with string lights and picnic tables. Pair with lunch on Sonoma Plaza or Napa's Oxbow Market (Hog Island oysters).
Late Aug–early Sep is crush season: grapes picked overnight, barrel tastings, harvest dinners and grape-stomps. Inland days run warm (~80s°F) but dry — a sunny contrast to foggy 'Fogust' SF.
Car-free verdict: arguably the smart way to do it (tasting + driving don't mix). The SF-pickup tour is zero-logistics; the Napa Wine Train is the upscale splurge. Book ahead — peak season.

BART from Embarcadero/Montgomery to Downtown Berkeley (~30 min), exit two blocks from campus — no car needed.
UC Berkeley + Sather Tower (the Campanile): Sproul Plaza, Memorial Glade, and the elevator up the 307-ft tower for Bay and Golden Gate views.
The 'Gourmet Ghetto' on North Shattuck: Chez Panisse (Alice Waters' farm-to-table birthplace), Cheese Board Collective pizza, the original Peet's.
Easy hill view: walk or bus up to Indian Rock Park for sunset over the Bay; Berkeley Marina for flat waterfront skyline views.
A chill food-and-views day, not big nature — Tilden Park is lovely but its weekend bus makes it a car-free stretch.

Stay Downtown / Belltown / Pioneer Square (Walk Scores 97–99): hotels steps from Pike Place, the waterfront and a Link light-rail stop; the flat core lets the parents wander car-free.
Pike Place → the new (2024) Overlook Walk → Waterfront Park: an accessible ramp-and-elevator bridge with 360° bay, Olympics and Rainier views; then Kerry Park for the postcard skyline.
Coffee-and-seafood downtime: third-wave roasters (Victrola, Lighthouse), bay-view oyster houses (Elliott's, The Crab Pot), and Ballard/Fremont breweries for the slow afternoon.
Fully car-free: Link light rail from the airport to downtown in ~38 min; ferries from Pier 52 cross Elliott Bay to Bainbridge in ~35 min — a scenic ride with zero driving.
Early September is Seattle's best window: warm-dry tail of summer, highs ~70–76°F, cool nights, almost no rain and the year's lowest humidity — exactly the heat-escape they wanted.

The 35-min Colman Dock walk-on ferry is the highlight — Seattle skyline, Elliott Bay, the Olympics, Rainier on a clear day. Free on the return.
Winslow's shops, cafés and restaurants start a flat 5-min walk off the boat — the whole core is walkable.
Wine & beer downtime on foot: Eleven Winery and Fletcher Bay tasting rooms, Bainbridge Brewing; lunch at Bruciato, Proper Fish or the Harbour Public House.
The Eagle Harbor Waterfront Trail is an easy ~1-mi paved stroll with benches and art; Bloedel Reserve gardens optional (timed reservation + shuttle).
Fully car-free: walk-on capacity is 2,000+, you're never turned away, and the BI Ride shuttle covers anything past Winslow.

Guided small-group day tour only — no transit reaches the park. Tour includes Seattle pickup, park fee and lunch (~$275pp), ~10–11h door to door.
Paradise (~5,400 ft) is the hub: the easy paved 0.5-mi Myrtle Falls stroll, the flat Reflection Lakes path, gentle meadow loops — scenic, not strenuous, ideal for the parents.
Early-Sep reality: peak bloom is mid-Jul–Aug, so meadows are fading but still scenic, with first fall color starting and thinner post-Labor-Day crowds.
The views: Rainier above Myrtle Falls, the mountain mirrored at Reflection Lakes, Tatoosh panoramas, Narada and Christine Falls en route. Crisp, cool, high air.
Verdict: the marquee big-nature day of the Seattle leg — but car-dependent, so a guided tour (or a rental) is mandatory.

A 268-ft thundering plunge, damn fine pie, and a little Twin Peaks magic.

A free paved ADA overlook of the 268-ft falls; an optional 0.7-mi trail drops to a misty river-level view at the base.
Salish Lodge & Spa sits right atop the falls — country breakfast since 1916, a relaxed lounge lunch, or pizza and honey cocktails in The Attic.
The lodge is the Twin Peaks 'Great Northern'; 10 min on is North Bend's Twede's Cafe (the Double R Diner) for cherry pie and 'damn fine coffee'.
Honest car-free: transit is rough (~90 min, ~3 transfers); best as a half-day guided tour or a 1-day rental that pairs the falls with North Bend and a valley winery.
Easy half-day verdict: genuinely low-effort scenery with real things to do; early Sep is warm, dry and quieter — go before 10am or after 3pm.

Hurricane Ridge — subalpine meadows and panoramic Olympic-mountain views; the Big Meadow and Cirque Rim loops are flat, paved, ~0.5 mi each. September = golden grasses, snow gone.
Hoh Rain Forest — the magical Hall of Mosses loop (~1 mi, easy) but ~4h+ each way; realistically an overnight, not a day trip.
Car-free reality (Hurricane Ridge only): 9:35a Bainbridge ferry → Strait Shot bus → Port Angeles → summer shuttle to the Ridge; or a guided tour (~$95pp + ferry/bus, ~9h). Long and tightly timed.
Lake Crescent — a turquoise glacial lake with the easy 1.8-mi Marymere Falls trail; the natural scenic lunch stop en route.
Honest verdict: biggest-wow, lowest-feasibility. Hurricane Ridge is the realistic car-free day (jaw-dropping, not strenuous) but a real haul. Included so you know we checked — swap it in for Rainier if the wild green calls louder.
